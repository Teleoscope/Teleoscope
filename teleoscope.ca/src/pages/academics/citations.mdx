# Citing Teleoscope

Teleoscope was developed as part of [Dr. Paul Bucci's](https://paulbucci.ca) PhD project while working with [Dr. Ivan Beschastnikh](https://www.cs.ubc.ca/~bestchai/) in [UBC's Systopia Lab](https://systopia.cs.ubc.ca/).

## Conference paper (cite this first)

**Crystallizing Schemas with Teleoscope: Thematic Curation of Large Text Corpora on Reddit** — *Proceedings of the 2026 CHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems (CHI ’26)*, Barcelona, Spain, 13–17 April 2026. ACM. [DOI: 10.1145/3772318.3791310](https://doi.org/10.1145/3772318.3791310) · [ACM Digital Library](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3772318.3791310)

If you use Teleoscope in academic work, please use the following [BibTeX](https://www.bibtex.org/) (primary):

```
@inproceedings{lee2026crystallizing,
  author = {Lee, Patrick Yung Kang and Bucci, Paul Hendrik and Foord-Kelcey, Leo Itsuki and Singh, Alamjeet and Beschastnikh, Ivan},
  title = {Crystallizing Schemas with Teleoscope: Thematic Curation of Large Text Corpora on Reddit},
  year = {2026},
  booktitle = {Proceedings of the 2026 CHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems},
  series = {CHI '26},
  address = {Barcelona, Spain},
  publisher = {Association for Computing Machinery},
  location = {New York, NY, USA},
  doi = {10.1145/3772318.3791310},
  url = {https://doi.org/10.1145/3772318.3791310},
}
```

Vancouver style (primary):

```
Lee PYK, Bucci PH, Foord-Kelcey LI, Singh A, Beschastnikh I. Crystallizing Schemas with Teleoscope:
Thematic Curation of Large Text Corpora on Reddit. In: Proceedings of the 2026 CHI Conference on
Human Factors in Computing Systems (CHI '26); 2026 Apr 13-17; Barcelona, Spain. New York: Association
for Computing Machinery; 2026. https://doi.org/10.1145/3772318.3791310
```

## Earlier arXiv preprint

An extended preprint with a closely related title (without the “on Reddit” subtitle) remains available: [arXiv:2402.06124](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.06124). Prefer the **CHI ’26 proceedings** citation above for the archival version of record once you need a single canonical reference.
